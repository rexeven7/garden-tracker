1. Basic Plant Care Logs (The Essentials)
These are the daily/weekly bread-and-butter logs that every gardening app needs.

💧 Watering: When was the last time the plant was watered?

Details to log: Method (drip, hand, rain), amount (light, deep), water source.

🍴 Fertilizing/Feeding: When was the plant fed?

Details to log: Fertilizer type (organic, synthetic, compost), N-P-K ratio, amount, method of application.

✂️ Pruning/Trimming: When was the plant cut back?

Details to log: Purpose (maintenance, disease control, shaping), amount removed.

🌱 Repotting/Transplanting: When did the plant move to a new home?

Details to log: New pot size, soil type used, root condition observed.

2. Growth and Development Logs (The Journey)
These logs help gardeners see the tangible progress of their plants over time.

📸 Photo Diary: The most vital visual log. Encourage users to take a photo from the same angle regularly.

📏 Measurement Log: How tall or wide is it getting?

Details to log: Height, width, number of leaves, number of blooms. A "graph view" of this data is highly motivating.

✨ Observation Notes: A general, open text field for anything else.

Examples: "First true leaves appeared," "looking a little pale," "new growth is vigorous."

3. Special Event Logs (The Milestones)
These track the major life events of a plant.

Sowing/Starting Date: When was the seed planted? (Indoors or directly outside).

Germination Date: When did the first tiny sprout appear?

First Bloom/Flower: When did the first flower open?

First Harvest: The big day! When was the first fruit or vegetable picked?

4. Problem and Treatment Logs (The Doctor’s Notes)
This section helps users troubleshoot and keep a history of issues.

🐛 Pest Identification: What’s eating the plant?

Details to log: Type of pest (aphids, slugs, mites), severity of infestation, location on the plant.

🍂 Disease Diagnosis: What are these spots?

Details to log: Symptoms (yellow leaves, powder, rot), possible cause (fungal, bacterial, viral).

💊 Treatment Log: What was done about it?

Details to log: Product used (neem oil, fungicide, insecticidal soap), frequency of application, effectiveness of the treatment.

5. Harvest and Yield Logs (The Reward)
Crucial for vegetable and fruit gardeners to track productivity.

Harvest Weight/Quantity: How much did they get?

Details to log: 3 tomatoes (total 1.2 lbs), 2 quarts of strawberries. A seasonal "total yield" view is a fantastic "flex" feature for users.

Taste/Quality Rating: A 1-5 star rating system for the harvest.

Examples: "Sweet and juicy," "too bitter," "small but flavorful."

6. Environmental and External Logs (The Context)
These logs help gardeners understand the "why" behind their plant's behavior.

🌦️ Weather Observations: A simple daily log or integration with a weather API.

Details: Temp (high/low), rainfall amount, "First Frost" and "Last Frost" dates.

☀️ Sunlight/Location: Is it getting enough light?

Details to log: Track how the sunlight changes in different parts of the garden throughout the season.

🧪 Soil Test Results: A place to log the pH, nutrient levels, and texture of the soil.

7. Inventory and Expense Logs (The Business of Gardening)
These help gardeners manage their resources.

🎒 Seed Box Inventory: A log of what seeds they have on hand.

Details to log: Variety name, brand, date purchased, expiration date.

💰 Expense Tracker: How much is this hobby costing?

Details to log: Plants, seeds, soil, tools, fertilizer, and where they were purchased.
